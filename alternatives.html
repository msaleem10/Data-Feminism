Counter-Data Alternatives

Comparative analysis and feminist redesign

Counter-Data Mapping

The problem of insider trading visibility—specifically the "Reported Silence" between trade execution and public disclosure—is the foundation of a multi-billion-dollar data industry. To understand how my Data Feminist audit differs from the prevailing market logic, I mapped my analysis against three counter-data projects that ingest the exact same raw SEC Form 4 filings.

Deep Dive: Most Compelling Alternative

If my data audit represents an attempt to expose the structural inequities of financial disclosure, Refinitiv represents the exact antithesis: a project designed to enclose, automate, and monetize those very inequities.

Project Overview

Project Name: StarMine Insider Filings Model US

Organization: Refinitiv (owned by the London Stock Exchange Group - LSEG)

Brief Description: Refinitiv provides elite institutional investors with predictive models based on raw SEC data. The StarMine model generates a 1 to 100 ranking of companies by analyzing the "Net Buyer Ratio" and "Selling Depth" of corporate insiders. Crucially, Refinitiv explicitly states that the model "exploits the information advantage of insiders to predict future stock performance."

Data Feminist Principles Analysis

1. Examine Power

Refinitiv does not view the 48-hour "Reported Silence" as a democratic failure, but rather as raw material for elite wealth extraction. By charging exorbitant fees for ultra-low latency API feeds, Refinitiv ensures the data is restricted strictly to hedge funds. It actively enforces a hierarchy of access where the retail public—whose 401(k)s absorb the resulting volatility—acts last.

2. Challenge Power

Instead of challenging the structural asymmetry of the SEC's 48-hour rule, the StarMine model entrenches it. It uses institutional infrastructure to validate the "Data Elite," turning the flaws of public disclosure laws into a proprietary data feed that concentrates wealth upward.

3. Elevate Emotion and Embodiment

The model completely sanitizes the human cost of market volatility. An insider sell-off that devastates a local economy or wipes out a working-class pension fund is completely stripped of its embodied impact, registered only as a "bearish signal" to be short-sold for profit.

4. Rethink Binaries and Hierarchies

Data Feminism warns against the reduction of complex realities into clean binaries. The StarMine model commits this erasure perfectly by flattening the chaotic, highly politicized reality of insider trading into a sterile 1-to-100 quantitative score. It hides the messy, unverified nature of self-reported SEC data behind a wall of proprietary mathematics.

5. Embrace Pluralism

The system is entirely monistic, optimizing strictly for one value: "Alpha" (market outperformance). It ignores pluralistic perspectives, such as the need for market stability, public accountability, or regulatory oversight.

6. Consider Context

The model erases vital context. For instance, the massive insider sell-off prior to the COVID-19 crash is not flagged by StarMine as a failure of public health transparency or a moral hazard; it is simply computed as a data point. It does not care why a trade was delayed or executed, only how it correlates to future price drops.

7. Make Labor Visible

Refinitiv capitalizes on the invisible labor of the public. The SEC's fragmented TSV files create friction for everyday citizens, but Refinitiv leverages its massive capital to bypass this friction entirely. It makes the labor of data cleaning highly visible only as a premium product to be sold.

Strengths & Limitations of the StarMine Model

Strengths (From a Capitalist Paradigm)

  • Highly efficient at identifying market trends before the retail public.
  • Outperforms basic models by 2.3% annually (creating massive value for clients).
  • Successfully overcomes the technical friction of the SEC EDGAR database.

Limitations (From a Feminist Paradigm)

  • Inherently extractive: "Alpha" for the elite means a guaranteed loss for slower, public-market participants.
  • Relies completely on the existence of structural inequality (the 48-hour lag) to function.
  • Completely devoid of ethical context or concern for systemic harm.


Feminist Redesign Proposal: The Pre-Trade Escrow

Based on the analysis of institutional extraction, I propose a redesign that shifts the regulatory architecture from a system of post-trade documentation to pre-trade transparency.

Redesign Vision

The current system heavily favors the "Data Elite" who perfectly comply with the 48-hour rule because it is optimized for their speed. Instead of allowing insiders to execute a trade and then enjoy a two-day window of opacity, the redesigned algorithm implements a Mandatory Pre-Trade Public Escrow. By forcing the elite to telegraph their movements, the algorithm neutralizes the information asymmetry that platforms like Refinitiv currently exploit.

1. Redesigned Algorithmic Logic Flow

Figure: The Pre-Trade Escrow Logic Flow

Key Changes

  • Change 1: The $1M Threshold Gate: The algorithm checks the transaction value. Standard employees trading under $1M proceed normally to avoid surveilling the working class.
  • Change 2: The 48-Hour Intent Escrow: If the trade is >$1M, the insider cannot execute immediately. They must file their intent, which halts the transaction for 48 hours.
  • Change 3: Public Notification Broadcast: During the escrow period, the intent is broadcast to a latency-equalized public ledger, allowing the market to digest the information before the capital is moved.

2. Refusal Statement

A core tenet of Data Feminism is recognizing that the decision of what not to build is as vital as what is built.

What We Refuse to Collect/Build:

  • Refusal 1: Predictive Algorithmic Alerts (Alpha Generation): I refuse to build APIs or push-notifications that correlate insider filings with future stock price predictions.
  • Refusal 2: Low-Wealth Granular Surveillance: I refuse to mandate the "Pre-Trade Escrow" for trades under $1,000,000, and I refuse to display the exact identities of low-level employees exercising standard stock options.

Refusal as Feminist Practice

Data meant for public accountability should not be repurposed into a proprietary weapon for high-frequency trading. Furthermore, subjecting lower-tier brackets to intense public scrutiny does not expose power; it simply gamifies the surveillance of the working class. True feminist data practice focuses the lens of accountability strictly upward.

3. Accountability Mechanisms

To prevent the redesigned algorithm from being captured by the same institutional forces, the following mechanisms are required:

Mechanism 1: Latency Equalization (The "Speed Bump")

What: An algorithmic throttle on API access.

How: The redesigned ledger will introduce a microsecond delay that ensures institutional data feeds (like Refinitiv) and retail web dashboards receive the Pre-Trade Escrow notifications at the exact same moment.

Who: Maintained by an independent SEC digital infrastructure team.

Mechanism 2: Plain-Text Translation Mandate

What: Removal of obfuscating financial jargon.

How: The algorithm will reject any filing that relies purely on technical footnotes. A machine-readable, plain-text explanation (e.g., "The CEO is selling 40% of their holdings") must accompany the code.

Who: Automated via natural language processing validation before acceptance.

Mechanism 3: Community Impact Audits

What: Annual review of the $1M threshold.

How: Funded by fines levied against non-compliant trades, an audit will determine if the threshold is effectively shielding the public's retirement funds from pre-shock liquidations without overburdening middle-management.

Who: An independent oversight board composed of public advocates and economists.

4. Stakeholder Map

Figure: Stakeholder map showing the quantitative shift in actionable access

Power Shifts Analysis

Who Gains Power?
  • Retail Investors: Gain temporal parity, allowing them to adjust positions before insiders liquidate.
  • Pension Funds (The Public): Gain the ability to protect middle-class retirement wealth from "mathematical aftershocks."
Who Loses Power?
  • Institutional "Data Elite" (Refinitiv/Hedge Funds): Lose the structural advantage of trading in the "Reported Silence."
  • Corporate Insiders (>$1M Trades): Lose the luxury of opacity and must face public scrutiny before securing profit.

5. Vulnerability Assessment

Identify the limitations and vulnerabilities of your redesign. No algorithm exists in a vacuum, and capital is highly adaptable.

Limitations

  • Limitation 1: Does Not Solve Systemic Poverty: Equalizing the speed of information does not equalize the capital required to act upon it. A retail investor knowing a CEO is selling stock is only empowered if they have the financial elasticity to adjust their own portfolio.
  • Limitation 2: Regulatory Capture: Wall Street lobbying groups possess immense power to dilute the implementation of the "Speed Bump," potentially re-introducing microsecond advantages.

Potential Vulnerabilities

  • Vulnerability 1: Capital Flight to Dark Pools: If the "Data Elite" cannot quietly liquidate non-derivative equity on public markets, they will likely transition their wealth into complex derivatives, offshore shell corporations, or private equity dark pools that fall outside the jurisdiction of this algorithm.
  • Vulnerability 2: Algorithmic Evasion: Insiders may begin breaking up $10 million trades into eleven $990,000 trades over a longer period to avoid triggering the $1M Pre-Trade Escrow threshold.
